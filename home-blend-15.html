On the Engineering of AI-Driven Tools for Bioinformatics Research

Peyk Engineering Practice

February 2026

Abstract

This document describes an engineering practice focused on the development of AI-driven software tools for bioinformatics research. We propose a model of embedded engineering: working alongside research teams to build machine learning pipelines, biological data platforms, and custom analysis tools. We outline three primary service areas and a three-phase engagement methodology designed to maximize knowledge transfer and research continuity.

1. Services

1.1 AI/ML Pipeline Engineering

We build and deploy machine learning models for genomic, proteomic, and clinical datasets. Our work spans sequence analysis, variant calling, protein structure prediction, and predictive modeling — always with an emphasis on reproducibility and production readiness.

1.2 Bioinformatics Platform Development

End-to-end platforms for managing, processing, and analyzing biological data at research scale. We focus on architecture that supports the volume and complexity of modern omics data while remaining operable by research teams.

1.3 Research Software Engineering

Custom applications, visualizations, and interfaces designed for researchers. We participate in the full lifecycle — from understanding the biological question to deploying tools that help answer it.

Fig. 1. Three service areas spanning the AI-bioinformatics software stack.

2. Methodology

Our engagement model follows three phases, each designed to minimize disruption to ongoing research while maximizing knowledge transfer.

2.1 Domain Assessment

We begin by understanding the research domain — the biological questions, the data types, and the existing computational infrastructure. The goal is an honest assessment of where software is limiting discovery.

We work across genomics, proteomics, transcriptomics, and clinical data modalities.

2.2 Embedded Development

We work as embedded engineers alongside your bioinformaticians and researchers, writing production code, training and deploying ML models, and building the platforms your research requires.

2.3 Transfer

Formal handoff including documentation of architectural decisions, training on deployed systems, and a post-engagement review to ensure your team can operate and extend everything independently.
